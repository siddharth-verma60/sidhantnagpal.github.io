State-wise contribution to GDP [1]

 

The state with most contribution to GDP is Maharashtra, followed by UP, Andhra P., Tamil Nadu, Gujrat and West Bengal. These six states contribute to more than 50% GDP of our nation.

 

 

There has been a steady rise in the total GDP of India.

 
But the percentage contribution of each state has remained approximately same in the past 14 years.
 

Sector-wise contribution to GDP of India [2]

It is interesting to note that Services and Manufacturing sectors have grown significantly, whereas Agriculture related sectors have dipped in the years from 1980-2010.
 

World GDP-per-capita (US $ / midyear population) [3]

 
There is very less increase in the GDP-per-capita of India and Pakistan, but China has started to rise since late 1990's. United States, on the other hand, are rising very steadily.
 

The drop in the GDP-per-capita of US during the 2008 depression is to be noted.

World GDP at market price (US $) [4]

 
The world GDP is rising at a steady pace. Since 2000's it has shooted up very fast except for the 2008 depression. But the pace was recovered in 3-4 years. Though since 2011 the rate has slowed down significantly.
 

India and Pakistan have slower growth compared to China and US. All four of these countries were affected by the 2008 depression, US was affected the most.
